Toronto District School Board

Advertising, Branding, Print Design

A sample of the work completed during my time as a Graphic Designer for the Toronto District School Board. I designed marketing assets, internal documents, promotional materials and templates for school use.

tools used

Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign, Microsoft Word

tasks

On the eve of the Toronto District School Board's rebranding, I joined a design team to create and update assets for the board and it's schools. My responsibilities ranged from editing photographs to designing entire brochures and posters for schools.

  1. Designed brochures for schools
  2. Created Microsoft Word templates for different schools
  3. Updated and redesigned internal documents
  4. Created and edited advertisements
  5. Edited photographs
  6. Liaised with fellow professionals

Final Products

Toronto Star Advertisement

Illustrator
8.97 x 20.00 in

The Toronto District School Board wanted a newspaper advertisement that promoted the multilingual afterschool programs offered at their schools. This full-page advertisement is lined with caricatures wearing traditional clothing or iconic symbols of different countries in which the language originated from. Each countries' respective flags are incorporated in some way on the clothing. Extensive visual research was completed to differentiate between countries and avoid misrepresentation. This advertisement was printed and distributed on the Toronto Star.

Cedarbrae Logo Design

Illustrator

Cedarbrae Collegiate Institute wanted a logo, different from their school crest, to match their modern needs. I focused this new identity based on their school mascot and horseshoe motif in the crest. The horse imagery also symbolizes strength, freedom, and progress. The colors herald from both the TDSB logo and Cedarbrae's crest.
