Breeze Fridge Project

Product, App & Poster Design

Breeze is a speculative app and fridge that will help users save their food, time and money. The fridge and app hosts a shared grocery list and an automated inventory list, ensuring that users are well informed about their groceries. Additionally, Breeze provides recipe suggestions using ingredients only found in the fridge.

tools used

Photoshop, Illustrator, Maya, Figma

the problem

Foodwaste at the household level is very wasteful for both the environment and the consumer. My household has always suffered from groceries being left and forgotten way past their expiry date.

How do we reduce unconsumed foodwaste at the household level?

research

Secondary Research

Studies have determined that food waste at the household level was the complex result of routine, preference, social expectation and convenience. Grocery shopping routines greatly contribute to food waste habits. A common hypothesis was the theory of planned behaviour where the intention and ease of doing something does not align.

Read the Research

Diary Study

A 7-day record and analysis of my food-related decisions. The diary alternates between using the 5 Whys method and a Cognitive Map. The resuts of the study matched the research findings; convenience is extremely important to the decision making process and only what is seen/known is what is considered.

Expiry Date Map

This is a visualization of the expiry dates of items in my fridge. Each box represents an item. The expiry dates listed above the items are ordered by depth-items placed farther back are listed first.

goals

  1. Keep users aware of what’s inside their fridge
  2. Reduce the likelihood of groceries being “buried”
  3. Encourage users to consume what’s inside their fridge
  4. Discourage purchases that leads to excess unconsumed groceries

Users must reduce excessive purchases and use what they have. To do so, we increase the user’s knowledge at various stages of the food buying, storing and consumption process in order to guide them towards developing anti-food waste behaviours.

process

Sketches

Personas

When designing the fridge, I considered two different audiences; one for the fridge and one for an informative poster. The primary audience is the end-user of the fridge. This could range from young children to adults. Basic requirements were being able to use a fridge and technological familiarity. The focus of the primary audience was to design a product that could reduce food waste. The secondary audience is geared towards those interested in reducing food-waste. The deliverable must entice the secondary audience to purchase the product.

Information Architecture

my solution

A fridge, armed with rotating shelves and scanners that can detect items within, and a mobile app that makes it convenient for households to keep track of their groceries, use them before they expire, and communicate while grocery shopping.

Easier access and constant visibility of the fridge’s contents

Motion sensor detects movement and lights up the large touch screen display. When idle, the display is dimmed to conserve energy and maintain a modern look.

Remove visual depth within the fridge

Ergonomic shelves that automatically rotate when the fridge is opened. It gives equal visual prominence to all items inside the fridge, ensuring that items are not forgotten. Plus, these shelves are color-coded so you know where everything is.

Remove user obstacles required for maintaining an accurate inventory

An array of scanners scans the entire fridge at programmable intervals, using barcodes and image recognition to automatically update your inventory list.

Cross-reference the needs of each member of the household

A communal shopping list that can be updated from both the fridge and app which flags users of duplicates purchases, referencing the inventory list. Includes editable groups of items to be easily add at later dates, adapting the app to user’s unique lifestyles.

Inform users of the possible meals in their fridge

Meal recommendations based off of what’s solely inside the fridge. Sortable by cooking time, number of ingredients used, and their expiry dates.

With a simple tap right from your fridge, tell members of your household that you’re cooking so they know to expect a home-cooked meal.

Clarify misconceptions around shelf life and expiration dates

Distinctions between Best Before, Expiry Dates and extended shelf life to remove confusion.

Plus, track previously bought items to highlight spending habits.

Get The Right notifications

Customize the behaviour of the fridge and app to suit your specific lifestyle needs whether it’s how early you receive notifications for expired food to the speed and rotation of your fridge.

Why don't current apps work?

Many grocery tracking and grocery list applications already exist. However, they require users to manually upkeep them. This considerable workload repels users with a busy lifestyle and becomes obsolete when not consistently maintained. BREEZE solves this issue by having the fridge do the work automatically.

the brand

Rationale

The term “breeze” is associated with lightness and easiness, highlighting how the fridge is meant to integrate into daily routine without impeding the user. The logo is inspired by the moving shelves that differentiate it from traditional fridges. The font is a modified sans serif, it’s rounded edges mimicking the shape of the shelves.

The brand aims to appear as non-invasive and unintimidating as possible. The fridge induces change and it is important for the experience to be feel seamless and easy. Due to it’s large and diverse audience, the brand must also maintain a wide appeal through simplicty. The focus is on it’s beneficial functions rather than leisurely, entrusting that it’s game changing features will satisfy its users.
