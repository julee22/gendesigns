The Period Purse

Brand Identity Redesign

The Period Purse is a charity organization that provides hygiene products for homeless menstruators. The rebrand strives to establish a stronger brand and improve the organization's digital and physical presence. It provides a professional yet welcoming identity to inspire trust with fellow members of the community.

tools used

Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop

the problem

The Period Purse, like many charities, rely on donations and community effort to thrive. If the organization is not deemed as professional or trustworthy, people will not feel comfortable donating to them. If people do not empathize with the charity's mission, which is reflected in it's image, they will also not support them.

How can The Period Purse be rebranded to establish a reliable yet empathetic identity for both recipients and potential donators?

research

Organization Values

Values

For all menstruators to experience healthy periods with dignity.

Missions

The Period Purse strives to achieve menstrual equity by providing marginalized menstruators with access to free menstrual products, and to reduce the stigma surrounding periods through public education and advocacy.

Tagline

Dignity. Education. Menstrual Health for All. Kindness, giving, love, community, dignity, educate.

Original Logo Critique

The original logo had erratic stroke thicknesses throughout. There were too many colors of similar hues, contributing to excessive visual noise. It was more complex than necessary, with details that did not translate well among different applications.

The typography was inconsistent; the one used for the logo, wordmark and website were all different. Although the cursive added a feminine and personable tone, it was nearly illegible in smaller applications.

Overall, the branding had little structure which made the charity seem unprofessional and unorganized.

**As of October 2020, The Period Purse has changed their logo. This critique was made in 2019.

Competitor Analysis

Research was conducted comparing the success and branding of organizations with similar goals—Helping Hearts & Handbags, I Support the Girls, and The Pad Project. The three organizations have vastly different tones to their branding. The Pad Project used emboldened and dramatic imagery, utiliz stark colors. Helping Hearts & Handbags, while most similar from an operational standpoint, had a website laid out like a written letter with quotes from Mother Teresa. It appeared very classy yet sincere. I Support the Girls had the most similar level of branding to The Period Purse. Their feminine approach matched the charity concept and goals.

goals

  1. Simplify the existing logo so that it is applicable across multiple mediums
  2. Establish a clearer and cohesive identity that reflects the charity's values
  3. Developing a distinguishing identity for donators and recipients
  4. Communicate the organization’s work in a way that maintains a recipient's dignity

Designing a cohesive and polished brand identity that maintains the warm vision of the charity while also increasing brand awareness through consistent visual motifs. Give the impression of a friendly and trustworthy yet responsible and legitimate charity organization.

process

The design process began with defining the brand’s goals and values. After generating a moodboard, an extensive number of purse icons and shapes were explored. The basic concept of the original logo was very clever, combining it's distinguishing feature with wordplay, but needed refining.

Moodboards

The keywords garnered from the charity were dignity and kind. I focused on items that are considered representative of women in mainstream media as a form 'feminine' dignity; things that make people feel 'pretty' or 'beautiful'. I referred to actual purse stores as well. Kindness showed softer tones and smaller happiness.

Exploration

The purse concept is what really differentiates this charity from other charities for the homeless. Thus, it was important to highlight the purse within the logo. A variety of shapes and abstractions of purses were explored. The circular form or period is a recurring theme to represent their mission of providing female hygiene products.

Iconography

Iconography would denote all the different items that are accepted by the charity. I experimented with different styles for use throughout their website and other printed mediums that will match the logo for a connected identity. The icons also served as background patterns.

The concept
Kate Spade meets Mother Theresa.

the brand

Rationale

The proposed rebrand balances strong typography with a feminine touch. It demonstrates the charity’s goal to reduce the stigma around periods, discussing the importance of menstrual education, while also understanding that the topic can be a delicate manner.

One feature was retained from the original logo- the singular red dot. The red dot is placed on the tittle or ‘i’ as a natural and subtler alternative while maintaining it‘s impact.

The word period is italicized to highlight it’s importance to the organization’s values and soften the texture of the slab serif.

After experimenting with different shapes for the icon, a wider purse was chosen with a heart-shaped flap. The wideness represents abundance and resembles the types of purses distributed to those in need.

The juxtaposition between strong and soft is extended to the brand colors which offset the bold blood red and charcoal on pastel pink and sky blue backgrounds.
